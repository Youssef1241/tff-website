Get to Know Us

Company Overview

We are an organization focused on supporting parenting, with special attention to mothers due to the vital role they play in raising children in our community. We are committed to educating and empowering parents, teachers, and caregivers to help them build functional families through our parenting courses and family development programs. We also work to empower children and equip them with authentic moral values through educational programs that aim to develop the child's character.


Our Ideas

  • 1) Mothers' Groups: "Step by Step"
    • These are support groups that help mothers express themselves, learn, and socialize on a weekly basis.
    • These groups aim to educate mothers and deepen their understanding of their children.
    • They are a space to open up topics and discuss parenting challenges and children's issues in depth.
  • 2) Mothers' Retreat Day (MOM RETREAT)
    • Another idea to support working mothers or those who cannot attend weekly sessions or morning meetings.
    • Held once a month.
    • During this day, an important topic is discussed, and it becomes an opportunity to answer many questions on mothers' minds.
    • At the end of the meeting, mothers can be divided into small groups for discussion and sharing parenting challenges.
  • 3) Basic Parenting Course
    • A training program delivered by psychiatrists.
    • It aims to educate and train parents on the foundations of raising children so they become effective.
    • Parents learn about parent-child relationship patterns, different parenting styles, child developmental stages, and sex education.
  • 4) Let's Play, Mama and Baba
    • Shared play between parents and children has a deeper value and meaning than we often imagine.
      "Play allows parents to enter the child's world on the child's terms, which strengthens closeness, trust, and bonding."
      "Play is the way children express themselves to us. It is also an opportunity to reconnect after any disagreement or argument. The more we join them in their world, the more cooperative they become when we ask them to join ours."

Dr. Marlene Bastawros

Founder of "Towards Functional Families"

She is a certified psychiatrist with an M.B.B.CH from the Faculty of Medicine, Cairo University, in addition to a Master's degree in Psychiatry and Neurology from Kasr Al Ainy School of Medicine. She also holds a Clinical Psychiatry Diploma from Al-Abbassia Mental Health Hospital.

She began her professional journey at El-Rokawy Mental Health Hospital, followed by positions at Al-Abbassia Mental Health Hospital and Behman Psychiatric Hospital, where she gained extensive clinical experience in mental health and psychotherapy.

Driven by her passion for strengthening family systems, she founded Towards Functional Families, an initiative dedicated to supporting healthy parenting and sound marital relationships through specialized programs designed for different family members.

Since 2009, she has led mothers' groups that have grown into a dynamic community. Over the years, these groups evolved into a team of mothers who now facilitate and train other mothers, creating a sustainable cycle of peer education and empowerment.

She has delivered tailored lectures and workshops for many major organizations and NGOs, including UN Women, UNFPA, and the National Council for Women. In addition, she regularly lectures teachers and parents in French and English schools across Cairo.
